# Gigit — Brand Identity (v0.1)

**Date:** June 2026. Companion to [`PRD.md`](../PRD.md) (§2 goals, §4 business model, anti-requirements) and [`research/competitive-landscape.md`](../research/competitive-landscape.md). The rule that governs this whole document: **every brand claim must be backed by a product policy we actually enforce.** Brand is how our policies become legible. If a value below ever loses its policy, cut the value from the copy.

---

## 1. The premise

Live entertainment booking for small venues runs on Instagram DMs, ghosted emails, handshake math, and getting paid "sometime." Everyone in it — the band, the bar, the sound tech — is a small operator doing business with other small operators, with no infrastructure between them.

**Gigit is the infrastructure: the place where the local scene does business.** Not a discovery app, not a talent agency, not a promoter. The boring, trustworthy machinery — confirmed bookings, automatic contracts, money that arrives when promised, a sound plan before load-in — under the most relationship-driven business there is.

The positioning gap (from the research): GigPig sells *ease* and joy; GigFinesse sells *concierge polish* with opaque pricing; Sofar got burned for opaque money; GigSalad/The Bash run race-to-the-bottom quote auctions performers resent. Nobody owns **square dealing** — the brand whose whole identity is that everyone at the table sees the same numbers and the deal holds. That's ours, because our product policies are the proof.

## 2. Brand idea in one line

> **Gigit — the square deal for the local scene.**

Working alternatives if "square deal" reads too retro: "Where the scene does business." / "Get the gig. Get paid." / "Book the night." The recommendation is to lead with **"Get the gig. Get paid."** on performer/tech surfaces and **"Book the night, every night."** on venue surfaces, keeping "where the local scene does business" as the company-level descriptor.

## 3. Core values (each with its enforcing policy)

1. **The money is on the table.** Every slot shows its budget. Every party on a booking sees what every other party is paid. We never set or algorithmically adjust anyone's pay. *(Policy: PRD §4.6 pay transparency; anti-requirement: no algorithmic pay-setting. Contrast: Sofar's opaque spread, GigFinesse's unpublished pricing.)*
2. **Free to be found. Forever.** No performer or tech ever pays to join, apply, or message. No pay-to-rank, no featured placement, no submission fees. Visibility is earned by showing up and playing well. *(Policy: PRD §4.1, F1.6. Contrast: Sonicbids' fatal tollbooth, ShowSlinger's paid tiers.)*
3. **A deal is a deal.** Confirmed means contracted, and contracted means paid — on schedule, with a written cancellation policy that protects both directions, and a real dispute process. Reliability cuts both ways: venues are reviewed on payment and listing accuracy just as acts are reviewed on showing up. *(Policy: F3.2/F3.3, F4.1/F4.2, F7.1.)*
4. **Sound is a third of the show.** Techs are bookable professionals on the same rails as everyone else — same feed, same contracts, same payment, same reviews — not a line item or a favor. We're the first platform anywhere to do this. *(Policy: F6; differentiator per research §8.)*
5. **Real people, on real stages.** We screen for fake and AI-fabricated acts; we never generate or "enhance" performer media; AI drafts paperwork, humans confirm everything binding. *(Policy: anti-requirements, F7.5, K9 invariant.)*
6. **The scene is the point.** We never own the show, the door, or the audience. We stay cheap forever — priced below the value of one filled slot — because the win condition is more live nights in more rooms, with the relationship belonging to the people in the room. *(Policy: PRD §2 non-goals, §4.5.)*

## 4. Key value proposition

**Master:** Gigit makes booking live entertainment a sure thing — the slot gets filled, the deal is in writing, the sound is sorted, and everyone gets paid what they saw up front.

**Per side:**
- **Venues:** *Fill your calendar without chasing anyone.* Post a slot in a text message; get real, reviewable acts applying with their actual sets; confirm with a contract and protection if they cancel. Cheaper than one round of drinks, provable in your register (eventually: literally — the ROI receipt).
- **Performers:** *More nights on stage, none of the chasing.* The venues that book acts like yours, visible at last, with budgets shown — no negotiating from zero, no essay applications, no pay-to-play. Paid same-week, automatically, every time.
- **Techs:** *Finally, a market for the work.* See the gigs that need sound near you, know exactly what you're walking into — room, inputs, set times — before you commit, and get paid like the band does: on the platform, on time.

## 5. Voice & language

**Register: a respected stage manager.** Knows everyone, wastes nobody's time, says numbers out loud, calm when the night goes sideways. Plain-spoken and warm, never chirpy; confident, never hyped. We write like we text — most of this product *is* texts.

**Principles:**
1. **Talk money like musicians do.** "$300 for two sets" — never "compensation," "remuneration," or coy ranges. If there's a fee, name the dollar amount and what it buys.
2. **Use the scene's words.** Gig, slot, set, room, the door, load-in, house PA, input list, rider, mark played. Say "the band," "the act," "the room," "your tech." Never "talent" as a noun for people, never "content" for someone's music, never "users," "supply," or "vendors."
3. **Short sentences. One ask per message.** SMS is a first-class surface; write everything as if it costs 160 characters.
4. **The spotlight is theirs.** We say "you" a lot and "we" rarely; Gigit is the rigging, not the headliner. No "we're thrilled to announce."
5. **Honest on the bad path.** Cancellations, disputes, failed payments get the calmest, clearest copy in the product — that's where the brand is actually earned. State what happened, what the policy says, what happens next, in that order. Never blame, never bury the fee.
6. **No hype vocabulary, ever:** revolutionize, disrupt, unleash, supercharge, rockstar/ninja, "magic," emoji pile-ups. One exclamation point per relationship, maybe.

**Voice samples:**

| Moment | Not this | This |
|---|---|---|
| Offer to performer | "🎉 Amazing news! A venue loved your profile!!" | "Lakefront Taproom offered you Fri Jun 26, 8–10pm, $400. Details and accept: {link}" |
| Slot confirmation (SMS) | "Your event has been successfully created!" | "Posted: Sunday brunch, acoustic, $200, 11–1. I'll text you when acts apply." |
| Venue cancels at 10 days | "Unfortunately your booking has been cancelled." | "Lakefront cancelled Fri Jun 26. Per the 14-day policy you're owed 50% ($200) — it's on the way, arrives by Tue. The slot's back in your feed if they repost." |
| Tech brief | "You have a new opportunity!" | "Sat at Burnhearts: 4-piece, vocals through house PA, 6 inputs, bring 2 monitors. Load-in 7, sets 9–12. $250." |
| Dispute opened | "Your case is under review." | "We've paused the payout while we look at this. You'll hear from a person within 5 business days. Here's what we're looking at: {link}" |

## 6. The relationship, side by side

**To performers, Gigit is the booking agent you could never afford — minus the 15%.** It watches for rooms that fit, puts your real work in front of them, papers the deal, and collects. It never gatekeeps (no curation wall, no fees), never negotiates your worth for you (you see the budget; you decide), and never pretends to be your manager, label, or path to fame. Promises we make: you'll never pay us; you'll never get paid late through us; you'll always know the number before you commit. What we ask back: show up, on time, as advertised.

**To venues, Gigit is the booker on staff you can't justify hiring.** It knows what fits your room and your budget, keeps the calendar full, handles paper and payment, and protects the night when an act falls through. It is *not* a talent agency curating taste for you — you pick the act, you own the crowd, the regulars are yours. Promises: the budget you post is the cost (plus a small, named fee — only once you're winning); cancellation protection is real; the acts are real people we've screened. What we ask back: pay through the platform, review honestly, treat acts like the professionals they are.

**To techs, Gigit is the first place the industry has ever put your chair at the table.** Twenty years of "ask around" replaced by a feed of gigs that need sound, each with the room specs and input list attached. Same contract, same payment timing, same review weight as the act. Promises: never a fee, never walking into a mystery, never chasing a check after the show. What we ask back: the rig you list is the rig you bring.

**Between the sides, Gigit is the neutral table.** We are nobody's agent against anybody. The transparency rules exist so that neutrality is checkable: everyone on a booking sees the same terms, the same money, the same reviews. When sides conflict, we follow the written policy, not the bigger account.

## 7. Naming & visual direction (light touch, v0)

- **"Gigit"** works as a verb — *"just gigit"* — keep that available; always one word, capital G, never "GigIt."
- Aesthetic lane: **gig poster, not SaaS.** Show-print typography, ink-on-paper contrast, real photography of real rooms (never stock stages, never AI imagery — that's a brand-level commitment, same as the product policy). Feels at home stapled to a telephone pole in Bay View.
- Color/energy: warm stage-light tones over dark room, not startup gradient. Looks right on a phone at 11pm in a loud bar.
- Trademark check on "Gigit" is still open (PRD §11.6) — settle before spending on identity assets.

## 8. Brand red lines (the don'ts that are really policies)

- Never show a gig without its pay. Never publish a fee without a dollar amount.
- Never charge, rank, or feature the supply side for money.
- Never use AI-generated imagery/audio of performers, anywhere, including our own marketing.
- Never claim an act "draws" without data (until the ROI loop can prove it, nobody can — including us).
- Never speak as the promoter: it's their show, their stage, their crowd. We booked the night; they made it.
